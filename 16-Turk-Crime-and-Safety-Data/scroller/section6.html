Safety vs. discomfort

While the available data supports that the 16 Turk Street residence hall is substantially more dangerous than other locations, student perceptions of the source of this danger and their personal risk can be shaped by stereotypes — specifically, anti-Black racism and stigma against homeless people. Many hallmarks of poverty and discrimination in the Tenderloin neighborhood, like young men playing loud rap music on the corner or people sleeping on the street, might threaten students’ comfort more than their safety.
